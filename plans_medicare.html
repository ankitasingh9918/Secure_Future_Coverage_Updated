What Are Medicare Insurance Plans?

Medicare is a federal health insurance program that was created to provide affordable healthcare for people 65 and older, as well as certain individuals under 65 who qualify due to disabilities. Medicare helps cover a wide range of healthcare needs, from hospital stays to prescription medications.

However, Medicare on its own does not cover everything. That is where Medicare Insurance Plans come in. These plans give you more control over your healthcare coverage by filling in the gaps and offering additional benefits. With Medicare Insurance Plans, you can choose the type of coverage that works best for your personal health, budget, and lifestyle.

Medicare is divided into parts, each covering different services:

Part A: Covers hospital care, inpatient services, and some skilled nursing care.

Part B: Covers medical services like doctor visits, preventive care, lab tests, and outpatient treatments.

Part C (Medicare Advantage): Combines Parts A and B into one plan and often includes drug coverage and extras like vision or dental.

Part D: Covers prescription drugs, which can make a big difference if you need regular medications.

By understanding the basics, you can start to see which plan or combination of plans may be right for you.

Types of Medicare Insurance Plans We Offer

At Secure Future Coverage, we specialize in helping you explore and compare the Medicare Insurance Plans available. Here are the main options:

Medicare Part B

Medicare Part B focuses on your everyday healthcare needs. It covers doctor visits, outpatient services, lab work, X-rays, preventive screenings, and certain medical supplies. If you want coverage for routine healthcare costs that come up outside of hospital care, Part B is essential.

Medicare Part D

Prescription drugs can be one of the biggest out-of-pocket healthcare expenses for seniors. Medicare Part D plans help lower these costs by covering a wide range of medications. Even if you are not currently taking many prescriptions, having a Part D plan can save you money in the future and protect you from late enrollment penalties.

Medicare Advantage (Part C)

Medicare Advantage, also called Part C, is an all-in-one plan offered by private insurance companies. It combines hospital coverage (Part A), medical coverage (Part B), and often prescription drug coverage (Part D). Many Medicare Advantage plans also include additional benefits such as dental, vision, hearing, or wellness programs. These plans can be a convenient option if you prefer having everything bundled together under one plan.
